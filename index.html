Mixing

Mixing is done either in Logic Pro X, or Ableton Live. If you already have vocal stems and multitrack files please see the step process below for sending them. Mixing is done at a professional level and is meant to allow your song to have more depth and color.

Guide for Multitrack files for mixing:

  • 1) Print your tracks without any reverb etc. Avoid inline processing like compression and EQ as much as possible. Remember to turn off reverb and delay effects on VST instruments before printing them.
  • 2) Consolidate all your tracks. Bounce them all from the same point within your session.
  • 3) Do all of your editing including vocal comps, copying choruses, auto-tuning, etc.
  • 4) Preferred format is WAV or Broadcast WAV, 24bits, 44.1kHz. If you tracked your song at only 16 bits, then keep it at 16 bits – do not try to convert the file. WAV files are higher quality than MP3 and allows for a cleaner more accurate mix.
  • 5) Please properly label tracks. Such as “piano”, “Synth1”, “Snare”, “Vox 1”. This will help save time.
  • 6) Please include the following when delivering your tracks: A *.txt file with notes including the file format for the tracks, tempo, name and contact info for the engineer and/or producer Also anything else such as what was used to record, problem areas, ideas, influences, etc. 
  • 7) ZIP all your files and upload them using www.wetransfer.com, www.sendspace.com (or a similar service) to jcoquendo13@gmail.com.
